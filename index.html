A year in the life of the gut microbiota

The human gut microbiome is a complex community of bacteria that inhabit our lower intestines and perform many functions important to human health. Research has focused on understanding what bacteria live in our guts, what they are doing and what it's affect is on the host health. To help understand how our gut microbiomes change over time, two researchers took samples of their own microbiome approximately once a day during 2011, published here. They sequenced the samples and identified which bacterial species were living in their gut at each point. In this visualization you can explore the structure of their gut communities, viewed at different phylogenetic levels.
